MOOCs

Higher education can also benefit greatly from the surge in educational software. One method of delivering online courses has been MOOCs (Massive Open Online Course). Over the course of one year, Coursera's online MOOC platform saw a 640% increase in users from 1.6 million to 10.3 million. MOOCs came into the limelight with 22 of the top 25 universities in the USA now boasting these types of degrees. Students from all over the world have the chance to earn a high-level degree only with a laptop and a stable internet connection.

Educational Software

Software such as Zoom, Google classroom, Moodle and other various education-based software saw a sudden and significant rise in usage. Many schools and universities opted to make use of their services. Zoom saw an increase in the number of daily meeting participants rise from 10 million in December 2019 to 300 million in April 2020. Zoom over the last 2 years has also seen its quarterly revenue inflate by 732%. An increase from $27 million in 2018 to $882 million in 2021. These huge increases were consistent with all education software, proving its popularity.

Like most other countries, Finland responded to the covid 19 pandemic. Finland closed their schools and adopted alternative methods of learning. The Finnish national agency for education made guides for schools to ensure an efficient learning process. They recommended using online learning platforms like Model, Zoom, Google Classrooms, Ville, and Teams. A variety of enhanced programs in Finland gave all students equal opportunities to learn.

The Finnish education and culture sector collaborated to create The Library of Open Educational Resources. Using it, students and teachers could access its open educational resources. Finna is a program set up by the Finnish government. With Finna, anyone could access information in Finnish museums, libraries, and other information archives without having to visit them.
These technology systems have revolutionized the way in which Finland delivers its education to students.
